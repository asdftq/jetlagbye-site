About Jet Lag

What causes jet lag?
Jet lag occurs when your internal circadian clock is out of sync with the local time zone. Your body's 24-hour rhythm controls sleep, hormones, metabolism, and other functions. When you travel across time zones quickly, this internal clock can't adjust fast enough, causing symptoms like fatigue, insomnia, and difficulty concentrating.
How long does jet lag last?
Without intervention, jet lag typically lasts 1-1.5 days per time zone crossed. So a 6-hour time difference could mean up to 9 days of symptoms. With proper circadian management using JetShift, you can significantly reduce this adjustment period.
Is jet lag worse going east or west?
For about 75% of people, jet lag is worse when traveling east. This is because advancing your clock (going to bed earlier) is harder than delaying it (staying up later). Your internal clock naturally runs slightly longer than 24 hours, making westward travel easier.
What are common jet lag symptoms?
Symptoms include difficulty sleeping at night, daytime fatigue, trouble concentrating, mood changes, digestive issues, and general malaise. Symptoms can appear immediately or within 12 hours of arrival and may worsen over the first few days.

Science & Methods

Why is light so important?
Light is the primary signal that resets your circadian clock. Bright light at the right time can advance or delay your internal clock by 1-2 hours per day. Light at the wrong time can make jet lag worse. This is why generic advice like "get outside" doesn't always work—timing is everything.
Should I take melatonin?
Melatonin can help when timed correctly. It's most effective for eastward travel and when taken 2-3 hours before your target bedtime. JetShift calculates optimal timing and suggests appropriate doses. Always consult your doctor before taking supplements.
What about caffeine and alcohol?
Caffeine can help with alertness but should be avoided 6-8 hours before your target sleep time. Alcohol may make you drowsy but actually disrupts sleep quality. JetShift includes caffeine cutoff times in your plan.
Do diet and fasting help jet lag?
Despite popular claims, there's limited scientific evidence that diet or fasting resets your circadian clock. These methods are based on rodent studies that don't translate well to humans. Light exposure remains the most effective intervention.

Troubleshooting

I'm not getting notifications
Make sure notifications are enabled for JetShift in your device settings. Go to Settings → Notifications → JetShift and ensure "Allow Notifications" is on. Also check that Do Not Disturb isn't blocking them.
My flight was delayed/changed
You can update your trip details at any time and we'll recalculate your plan. Just edit your trip from the main screen and the new times will sync immediately.
The plan seems wrong for my situation
Our plans are based on typical circadian patterns. If you're a strong "night owl" or "morning person," your experience may vary. You can adjust your chronotype in settings for more personalized recommendations.
